Step 4 of 7
Walk-Forward Validation
Why walk-forward? Why not random split? This page explains the critical difference — and why getting it wrong means your model is useless in production.

⚠️ Data Leakage Warning — Random Split is WRONG for Time Series

Random train/test split shuffles the data — your model trains on future observations and is tested on past ones. This is called temporal data leakage. The model has already "seen" the answer before being asked the question. The result is artificially low error metrics that completely fall apart in production.

Walk-forward validation (expanding window) enforces temporal integrity: at every step, training data is strictly before test data — exactly as in real deployment. The metrics are honest.

❌ Random Split (Dishonest)
—
RMSE — falsely optimistic
Trains on future, tests on past. The model "cheats" by having already seen the test period during training. Useless in production.
✅ Walk-Forward (Honest)
—
RMSE — reflects real deployment
Always trains on past only. Each prediction is made without seeing future data. The error you see is the error you get in production.

Step 7 of 7
Methodology
Every decision — what was used, why it was used, and what the alternative was. Written to be understood by both technical interviewers and business stakeholders.
1

Dataset Design Python · NumPy

Generated a realistic Rossmann-style retail sales dataset: 5 years daily (2018–2023), 3 stores, weekly seasonality (Mon–Sat), annual seasonality (Christmas peak, Jan trough), promotions (+15% lift), public holidays (–60%), and a COVID-19 structural break in 2020. All characteristics are grounded in real-world retail behaviour documented in published Kaggle competition analyses.

2

Exploratory Data Analysis Pandas · Plotly

Decomposed variance across dimensions: day-of-week (weekly pattern), month-of-year (annual seasonality), year-over-year growth, promotional uplift. Rolling statistics (28-day mean and std) characterised non-stationarity and volatility regimes. Promo and holiday effects were quantified separately before being incorporated as features.

3

Seasonal Decomposition (Additive) NumPy · Custom

Implemented additive decomposition (Y = T + S + R) from scratch using a centred moving average for trend extraction (period=7). Seasonal indices computed as period-position averages of the detrended series, centred to sum to zero. Chosen over multiplicative because the seasonal amplitude is stable regardless of sales level (holiday patterns are additive shifts, not proportional multipliers).

4

Holt-Winters Triple Exponential Smoothing Custom Python

Implemented from scratch without statsmodels. Three components: Level (α=0.3), Trend (β=0.1), Seasonal (γ=0.2). Additive seasonality with period=7. Initialised via first-period averages. Chosen as a strong statistical baseline for seasonal series — interpretable, fast, and robust to missing data. Parameters fixed at typical values for simplicity; production use would optimise via grid search or MLE.

5

Gradient Boosting Regression Scikit-learn GBR

Feature-engineered calendar regression: cyclical encoding of day-of-week, month, and week-of-year (sin/cos pairs), lag features (1, 2, 3, 7, 14, 21, 28 days), rolling means and std (7-day, 28-day), and external regressors (Promo, StateHoliday, SchoolHoliday). n_estimators=80, max_depth=4, learning_rate=0.08. Captures non-linear interactions between features — e.g., Saturday × December × Promo is a distinct regime that linear models miss.

6

Seasonal Naïve Baseline Python

Predicts each future day as the value from exactly one week ago (same day of week). This is the industry-standard baseline for seasonal series — any model that cannot beat seasonal naïve is not worth deploying. Both HW and GBR beat the naïve baseline, validating their use.

7

Walk-Forward (Expanding Window) Validation Custom CV

Critical design decision. Training window expands from a minimum of 180 days, predicting the next 7 days, then advancing. This is repeated across the final 90-day test period. No future data ever appears in the training window. Random split was explicitly rejected: shuffling a time series breaks temporal structure, causes data leakage (training on future to predict past), and produces falsely optimistic metrics that collapse in production.

8

Forecast with Prediction Interval Holt-Winters + residual σ

30-day ahead forecast generated by Holt-Winters fitted on the full series. Prediction interval computed as point forecast ± 1.96σ, where σ is the standard deviation of residuals from the last 90 days (recent residual window). This is a parametric PI assuming normal residuals — appropriate here given the near-normal residual distribution confirmed in the decomposition analysis.
